# Project Origin & Motivation

Scarlet bought a Bambu X1 Carbon last year but health issues, a move, and the intensity of building aswritten meant it sat largely unused. Taylor's recent excitement about the printer reignited Scarlet's interest in generative 3D work.

Several motivations are converging:

## A creative project that isn't aswritten

Scarlet wants something purely creative — a counterweight to the business she's building. Aswritten needs to move fast, and that sometimes means doing things "dumber and faster." A separate project gives her room to breathe.

## An experimental ground for AI workflow

This project is deliberately a sandbox for pushing her Claude Code practice — trying multi-agent systems, new agent coordinators (Martin's factory-factory, Conductor), workflow patterns she doesn't want to risk on aswritten's velocity. The generative pipeline is the content; the AI collaboration process is a parallel interest.

## AI unlocking previously inaccessible work

This is a major pull. Projects Scarlet was fascinated by — 3D Manhattan Voronoi tessellation, cochlear modeling, other work where the math exceeded what she could implement alone — are now accessible with AI assistance. "A lot of the projects that were previously kind of over my head or that I was frustrated by not really being able to get all the way to with the software component or the generative output component are now something that I could do much more fluently with AI."

## Clojure and Karsten Schmidt

Clojure is a deliberate choice, not just familiarity. Karsten Schmidt's work (thi.ng) is what got Scarlet into programming. She wants to see what forms emerge from functional, procedural generative processes using thi.ng/geom — and she's excited to return to that lineage after 13+ years of professional Clojure development.

## Taylor

Not approaching this as a shared project, but Scarlet wants creative intersection — a world they're both interested in where she can help Taylor with CAD and they can find overlap organically. Taylor is self-sufficient with the printer but hasn't done 3D modeling yet.
