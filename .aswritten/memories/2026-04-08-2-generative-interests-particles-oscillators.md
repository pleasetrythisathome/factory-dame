# Generative Interests — Particle Systems, Emergent Structure, and Oscillator Networks

Scarlet's generative interest isn't primarily about Voronoi. Voronoi is one *derivation* from the deeper interest: **particle systems and emergent behavior**.

## Particle Systems as Foundation

Scarlet's entry into programming was through particle systems — specifically flocking simulations (boids, organic motion). The fascination is with systems where individual agents respond to input and to each other, producing emergent behavior. The structural forms that can be derived from these systems — Voronoi tessellation being one — are outputs, not the point.

She wants to build out particle systems as the generative foundation, then layer different structural derivations on top:
- **Voronoi** — subdividing space between particles
- **Shards** — geometry representing particle movement vectors and direction
- **Manhattan/Lp-metric Voronoi** — alternative distance metrics producing different cell geometries from the same particle positions

## Nonlinear Oscillator Networks

A separate but conceptually linked interest: using coupled nonlinear oscillator networks (inspired by Edward Large's cochlear dynamics research — Lerud, Kim, Almonte, Carney & Large, *Hearing Research* 2019) to drive generative forms. The specific paper models cochlear mechanics as coupled oscillators at Hopf bifurcation points, producing emergent harmonic behavior from individual tuned elements.

Scarlet's application: oscillator networks with large parameter sets — analogous to individuals in a particle system — that are harmonically linked to input (audio, music). The output drives music visualization or textural/sculptural forms. The throughline from particle systems: emergent behavior from interacting agents with local rules, but in the frequency domain rather than spatial.

Reference: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6669083/ (Large may have other related papers Scarlet was thinking of)
